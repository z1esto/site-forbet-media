Our solutions

Three mobile concepts for the modern sports fan.

We are working on three product lines, each addressing a different part of the modern fan's day — from morning headlines to live match nights to long-form post-game analysis.

Concept 01 — Media

A clean sports media aggregator.

A mobile reader that brings together news, match reports, transfer rumors and long-form editorial pieces from a curated list of trusted sports publishers. The reading experience comes first — typography is generous, navigation is quiet, and the feed is structured around topics the user actually follows.

The app does not write its own journalism. It surfaces what is already out there and respects the original sources with clear attribution and direct links back to publishers.

  • Topic-based personalized feed
  • Offline reading and saved articles
  • Daily morning digest notifications
  • Publisher-friendly attribution and linking

Concept 02 — Data

A statistics app for people who want context.

Most stats apps overwhelm the user. Ours does the opposite. It picks a small number of meaningful metrics, presents them with proper visual hierarchy, and explains what each one actually measures.

Built for football, basketball and tennis to start, with a clean modular architecture so additional sports can be added without rewriting the foundation. Real-time updates where possible, sensible polling intervals where not.

  • Live match center with timeline
  • Player and team profile pages
  • Historical head-to-head comparisons
  • Lightweight, fast, no clutter
Concept 03 — Community

A fan platform built around matchday.

A lightweight community app that wakes up around fixtures and goes quiet between them. Fans can follow their clubs, set up alerts, vote in polls, and chat about specific matches in well-moderated rooms that close when the final whistle goes.

We deliberately avoid the "always-on" social model. Sport has a natural rhythm — our product follows it instead of fighting against it.

  • Fixture-bound discussion rooms
  • Club and competition following
  • Pre-match and full-time polls
  • Strong moderation and reporting tools
